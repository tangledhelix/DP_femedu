###About the Book

[Emma Willard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emma_Willard) - 1787-1870 - founded the first institution for higher education for women, which is now a prep/boarding school in New York named after her. She wrote this pamphlet in an attempt to persuade the state legislature to publicly fund women's education and to focus on more rigorous material.

Printed in 1918 (a reprint of the original 1819 pamphlet).

### Spellcheck ###

errour (p. 15): Obsolete spelling of `error` per [Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/errour), [Johnson's Dictionary](https://johnsonsdictionaryonline.com/1755/errour%5Fns).

existencies (p. 15): Plural of `existency`, which is an archaic form of `existence`, per [Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/existency#English).

### Transcriber's notes ###

p. 15: archaic spellings “errour”  and “existencies” left as printed.

p. 28: changed “benificial” to “beneficial” (most beneficial control over common schools)
